R Youngblom

Audio

A.A. Arts and Humanities—Broadcast Electronic Media Arts; CCSF

City College of San Francisco is one of my favorite institutions on the planet, and I will probably never stop taking classes there. I studied sound recording in the BEMA department as part of my A.A. while running a home studio out of my garage. Sound is my passion—some of my recording projects can be found on Soundcloud.

Law

J.D. Intellectual Property; U.C. Davis

I earned my law degree and Intellectual Property certificate from U.C. Davis School of Law (King Hall). While at King Hall, I worked on the Business Law Journal, was a member of the Intellectual Property Association, and co-founded the Disability Rights Law Association.

Philosophy

M.A. Philosophy; SFSU

I received a Master's of Arts in philosophy from San Francisco State University. My thesis was entitled "Bodily Autonomy in Confucian Thought" and looked at bioethics—specifically bodily autonomy—through a Confucian lens. A copy of my thesis can be read on Google Docs.

Coding

B.S. Computer Science; OSU (2019)

I'm currently working on a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science at Oregon State University. I'm taking advantage of their professional patent bar training track, which entails a lot of remote work, as well as significant on-campus labs and science courses. I will be graduating in Summer 2019, and a lot of my work is on Github.

Ocean Source

Sustainably-Harvested Kelp Pickles

One of my side projects is running a company called Ocean Source. We are currently lobbying the state of California to allow us to build carbon-negative seaweed farms off the coast. In the meantime, we harvest bull kelp and make Sweet & Spicy Kelp Pickles! You can learn more about us here.

Prototype Penguin Synthesizer

My adorable neighbor gave me a small penguin toy just as I was building a little synth on an Arduino. Opened the penguin, put in the Arduino, put some pots on the box, and... say hello to Penguino, your playable synth friend! It's not exactly a Nord, but it makes the noises I want it to, mostly. Working on building a better case (as I shredded the box somehow) and practicing playing a little Taylor Swift. Sounds pretty good!
